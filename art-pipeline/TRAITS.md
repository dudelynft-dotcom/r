# ROBARK — Trait Bible

**Style law (applies to every trait, every NFT):** ROBARK is *hand-drawn*, not
rendered. Think OK Guy / indie sketchbook: a thick **black marker outline of
uneven, wobbly thickness**, **flat felt-tip fills** (no gradients, no airbrush,
no 3D, no glossy shading), slightly **off-register coloring** like a human filled
it in, naive doodle proportions, faint paper texture. If a trait ever looks
clean, smooth, symmetrical or "digital," it's wrong. It must look **drawn by a
talented human, never by AI.**

The base character is always recognizable: a droopy-eared dog, big round black
nose, heavy tired eyes, slouchy "lost everything but still bullish" energy. Only
the traits below change between pieces.

There are **9 trait layers** plus **1/1 Legendaries**. Combination space is in
the millions, so all 10,000 are unique. Rarity % is the share of the 10k that
should carry each value (tune in `traits.yaml`).

## How variation reads across the collection
Every NFT is the **same dog** rendered in the **same handmade hand** — but the 9
layers above recombine so that fur, eyes, mouth, head, eyewear, outfit, chain,
accessory and the burning world behind him are different on every single piece.
Two ROBARKs are never twins: one is a cream dog in a black suit, shades up,
cigarette, gold chain, hands in pockets, red candles crashing; the next is a
chocolate dog in a bathrobe, X-eyes, blunt, halo, trash-bag portfolio, standing
in a rug-pull crater. Same soul, 10,000 different bad days.

> Rarity tiers (Common → Rare → Epic → Legendary → Mythic) are derived from the
> combined frequency of a piece's traits in `metadata.py`, with the 1/1s on top.
